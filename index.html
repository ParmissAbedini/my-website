01

Medical Device · Capstone Project

Self-Reducing Wrist
Fracture Device

A portable mechanical system designed to assist wrist fracture reduction through controlled traction, independent of hospital beds or external mounting. Developed for emergency rooms, clinics, and remote environments with a focus on structural integrity, adjustability, and patient safety.

SolidWorks FEA Prototyping FMEA DHF Traction Mechanism

Alpha prototype of the wrist traction device applying controlled linear traction to the forearm using a winch-driven telescopic mechanism.

Mechanical testing of the wrist traction device demonstrating sustained ~120 lbs traction for 20 minutes with no structural failure; results used to inform ongoing refinement of the mechanism, material selection, and integration of end-user feedback.

Full device kit including hand attachment, straps, and carrying case.

Finite Element Analysis (FEA)

Finite Element Analysis (FEA) was conducted in SolidWorks Simulation to evaluate structural performance under applied loading conditions. The base plate was fixed, and a 110 N load was applied to the traction rod. Stress and displacement outputs were analyzed to assess structural integrity and identify critical stress concentrations.

Von Mises stress — Max 5.302×10⁵ N/m², below material yield limits.

Displacement — Max 3.094×10⁻³ mm under 110 N load.SolidWorks CAD render — front view showing clip slots

02

Medical Device · Design Engineering

IV Line Organizer

A 3D-printed, clip-based IV line management device designed to reduce line tangling and improve clinical workflow efficiency. The device attaches securely to standard IV poles and organizes multiple IV lines in a compact, accessible configuration. Three size variants were developed to accommodate different line densities while supporting standard IV catheter sizes (20G–24G). The design prioritizes manufacturability, ease of distribution, and intuitive operation, enabling rapid adoption in clinical environments with minimal user training.

03

Orthopaedic Implant · Structural Engineering

Distal Femur Implant

Design of an internal fixation implant for an 88-year-old patient with a distal femur fracture requiring immediate weight bearing who could not tolerate prolonged immobilization. The design needed to provide high mechanical stability, support full body weight loading, accommodate bone fragility due to age and osteoporosis, and remain compatible with a previous unicondylar knee replacement while minimizing surgical invasiveness and promoting bone healing.

SolidWorks FEA Orthopaedics 3D Printing Structural Analysis

SolidWorks CAD model — distal femur fixation plate with screw configuration.

Lateral plate CAD drawing with dimensions and screw hole configuration for distal femur implant design (cm).

FEA von Mises stress test of the structural plates (SolidWorks Simulation). Max stress well below yield strength of 8.274×10⁸ N/m².
